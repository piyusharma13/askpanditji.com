Our Dharmic Promise to You

Following the Sanatan anushashan (discipline) of our lineage, we view dakshina not as payment but as a willing offering of gratitude and bhakti.

When the puja is done, when the mantra has been recited and the blessings received — the offering, whatever it may be, half of it goes directly into seva: the daily care of Thakur Ji, gau sewa, and keeping our mandirs alive for all who come.

We also follow one firm Vedic discipline: kundali readings are conducted only during auspicious daytime hours — never after sunset.

Come with your prayers. Leave with what you feel is right.

About

Rooted in Amritsar,
Serving the World

I am Pandit Sanyam Sharma, the 4th generation in our sacred family lineage, son of late Pandit Shri Parveen Kumar. For over 120 years, our family has been serving the divine at two mandirs in the holy city of Amritsar. We are also the kul purohit (family priests) for esteemed families in Himachal Pradesh and Punjab. What began as local seva has grown — devotees from Delhi, Mumbai, and even Canada and the USA now receive blessings through our services.

We believe that true dharma asks for nothing in advance. When seva is done with full heart and the devotee feels the blessings — only then does the dakshina feel right. This is our way.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked
Questions

Can I book an online puja from outside India?

Yes. Pandit Sanyam Sharma offers online puja and jyotish consultations to devotees worldwide, including the USA, Canada, the UK, and the Middle East via video call.

When do I have to pay for the puja?

You pay only after the service is complete and you are fully satisfied. There is no advance payment required.

Can Pandit Ji travel for an in-person puja or ceremony?

Yes. In-person puja and ceremony visits can be arranged depending on the date and location. If travel outside Amritsar is required, suitable travel and accommodation support will also be needed. Please contact in advance on WhatsApp or phone to confirm availability and arrangements.
