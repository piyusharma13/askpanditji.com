Our Dharmic Promise to You

Following the Sanatan anushashan (discipline) of our lineage, we view dakshina not as payment but as a willing offering of gratitude and bhakti. Every offering received is sacred—we prepare and offer bhog to Thakur Ji with full devotion, then share these blessings with our devotees and community. This is our ancient practice: honor the divine first, then extend grace to all. We also support gau sewa (cow care) and the daily activities of our mandirs through these offerings. Kundali readings are conducted only during auspicious hours—never after sunset, as per Vedic practice.

About

Rooted in Amritsar,
Serving the World

I am Pandit Sanyam Sharma, the 4th generation in our sacred family lineage, son of late Pandit Shri Parveen Kumar. For over 120 years, our family has been serving the divine at two mandirs in the holy city of Amritsar. We are also the kul purohit (family priests) for esteemed families in Himachal Pradesh and Punjab. What began as local seva has grown — devotees from Delhi, Mumbai, and even Canada and the USA now receive blessings through our services.

We believe that true dharma asks for nothing in advance. When seva is done with full heart and the devotee feels the blessings — only then does the dakshina feel right. This is our way.
